The Hard Part Is the Finish

Anyone Can Patch a Hole. Matching It Is the Skill.

Drywall repair looks straightforward until you try to do it yourself — and end up with a visible patch that no amount of paint will hide. The real challenge isn't filling the hole: it's feathering the compound smooth enough and matching the surrounding texture closely enough that you can't see where the repair ends and the original wall begins. That's a skill that comes from doing it repeatedly, on walls with every kind of texture from smooth skim coat to knockdown to orange peel.

Justin at VixFix handles drywall repairs of all sizes across New Hampshire — from nail pops and small impact holes to full panel replacements after water damage or plumbing work. Every repair gets finished to the level the wall demands: sanded smooth where the wall is smooth, texture-matched where it needs to blend in.

Drywall Services We Provide

🕳️

Hole Patching

Small holes from doorknobs, anchors, or accidents patched with the right backing method for the size — mesh tape, California patch, or backer board — then finished flush to the wall.

💧

Water Damage Drywall Repair

Saturated or stained drywall removed, the cavity dried, new panels hung and finished. Stain-blocking primer applied before paint so the damage doesn't bleed through later.

🔩

Nail Pop & Crack Repair

Nail pops and settling cracks reset, filled, and feathered. A finish that looks the same as the surrounding wall — not a visible bump or line running across the ceiling.

🖐️

Texture Matching

Orange peel, knockdown, skip trowel, smooth — Justin matches the existing texture by hand or with the right spray technique. The hardest part of drywall repair, done right.

🏗️

Full Drywall Installation

New rooms, basement conversions, or full room redo after damage or renovation. Hung, taped, mudded, sanded, and finished to the level required before painting.

🎨

Pre-Paint Prep & Prime

New or repaired drywall primed before paint so color applies evenly and the sheen matches throughout the room. Skipping primer on new drywall causes flashing — Justin doesn't skip it.
