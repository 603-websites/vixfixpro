Water Damage Gets Worse the Longer You Wait

New Hampshire homeowners know the drill: a frozen pipe bursts in January, ice dams force meltwater through the roof in February, or a bathroom leak quietly saturates the ceiling below for weeks before anyone notices. Whatever the source, water damage in the interior of your home needs to be addressed quickly, saturated drywall deteriorates, mold can establish within 48–72 hours, and stains left untreated will bleed through any number of paint coats.

Justin at VixFix handles water damage repairs from start to finish. That means identifying the source if it hasn't been fixed yet, removing damaged material, drying the cavity, patching and finishing to match the existing surface, and painting so the repair is invisible. No handoff between a "demo crew" and a "finish crew", one person handles the whole thing.

Water Damage Repair Services We Provide

🪣

Ceiling Water Damage Repair

Sagging, stained, or bubbled ceiling drywall cut out and replaced. Stain-blocking primer applied before any finish coat, so the stain doesn't come back through six months later.

🧱

Drywall Removal & Replacement

Saturated drywall removed to dry framing, cavity allowed to dry fully, then new drywall hung, taped, mudded, and finished to match the existing wall texture.

🏔️

Ice Dam Damage Repair

Ice dam infiltration leaves a distinctive pattern of staining along exterior walls and ceiling perimeters. Justin understands how NH ice dams work and repairs the interior damage correctly after the dam is addressed.

🚿

Bathroom & Plumbing Leak Repairs

Slow leaks from shower surrounds, supply lines, and drain seals often go undetected until the ceiling below shows damage. We repair what the leak damaged, walls, ceilings, and floor surfaces.

🪵

Subfloor & Flooring Repair

Water that reaches the subfloor can cause buckling, soft spots, and mold. Damaged subfloor sections replaced and finished flooring reinstalled to match the surrounding floor.

🎨

Paint & Finish Restoration

After structural repairs are complete, priming and painting to match the surrounding surfaces. The goal is a repaired area you can't distinguish from the original, not a visible patch.
